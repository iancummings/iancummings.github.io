The central character is a maid and she is autistic. There's nothing wrong with that of course, but the author obviously wanted to give us a feeling of what autism is like for some people (autism is many things to many people) although nobody actually says the word in the book. Sadly it felt to me like the author had made a list of neurodivergent traits from a 10 minute internet search. The resultant slog through the book really did get me down; this is supposed to be a murder mystery but the plot is very plodding and formulaic.

The ending is what finished it for me; it seemed to upturn all of what Molly said she stood for.
I shan't be reading the next in this series.
